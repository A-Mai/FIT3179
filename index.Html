Happiness Around the World in 2021

Happiness Index of Countries around the World

The World Happiness Index is a ranking of national happiness based on the Cantril Ladder survey (thus the ladder score). Respondents rate their life on a 0 to 10 scale, with 0 being the worst possible life and 10 being the best possible. Thus country rankings don't actually go to 0 or 10 as they are averaged scores. The highest ranked country is Finland with a score of 7.842 and the lowest ranked country is Afghanistan at 2.523

From this graph, outliers when it comes to state happiness in a region can be found. Venezuela and Haiti are uniquely unhappy in their geographic region while Khazakstan, Uzbekistan, Saudi Arabia, United Arab Emirates, and Thailand are happier than their neighbours.

Happiness Index Against GDP Per Capita (Logged)

Directly from the graph, it can be seen that there is a correlation between happiness and GDP per capita, as well as healthy life expectancy. When adjusting the sliders, one can also see that there is some correlation with corruption perception and social support with the other factors.

Interesting outliers that can be seen when interacting includes Rwanda, with a low happiness index and GDP per capita, yet is extrodinarily low in corruption perception at second lowest behind Singapore.

Average Data by Region
The average data presents similar findings to that of the scatterplot graph above, mainly that North America and ANZ have the highest happiness on average followed by Western Europe. This is despite the Western European region having some higher happiness countries, they are more spread out.
